# Andrew Pickering

> Head chef of a kitchen that cooks 2,000 covers a day. Loves a spec sheet.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Head Chef, at a flagship enterprise pub (same group as Liam Henderson, Priya Sharma, Karl Becker)
- **Age**: 44
- **Location**: London, United Kingdom
- **Venue**: A 600+ capacity flagship. Kitchen produces around 2,000 covers a day across lunch, dinner, and late food. Menu is centrally defined.
- **Reports to**: The venue GM and, on menu and food strategy matters, a central Group Executive Chef.
- **Manages**: A kitchen of 22 across two shifts. A sous chef per shift, CDPs, commis, KPs.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Enterprise chain, 1 of 620 venues, at a flagship site
- **Service style**: Pub and hotel
- **Geography**: United Kingdom, London
- **Tech maturity**: Functional
- **Buying motion**: Procurement-led (does not choose tools)

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: bepoz at venue level with a KDS configured for high-throughput multi-station kitchens.
- **Solutions they touch**: KDS, central recipe management, stock and ordering (centrally controlled), labour scheduling.

---

## Bio

Andrew did a culinary apprenticeship straight out of school, came up through hotel kitchens, moved into the pub group at 31, has been head chef at three different venues across the group. He has been at this flagship for four years.

He does not invent menus. The menu is set centrally by the Group Executive Chef and rolled out to all 620 venues with spec sheets, photos, and training videos. Andrew's job is to deliver the menu, at consistent quality, at the throughput the venue demands, on the GP% the group expects.

He is precise. He is a brand operator first and a creative second. He is also the person who tells the Group Executive Chef when a centrally-designed dish does not survive a Saturday at scale.

---

## A day in the life

A Saturday with a match on.

- **08:00**: Arrives. Walks the deliveries. Spec sheets in hand.
- **09:30**: Morning brief with the sous and the leads. Allocates stations.
- **11:00**: Kitchen prep is full pace. He is on the pass for the lunch.
- **12:00**: Lunch peak. KDS handling 90 covers an hour smoothly.
- **15:00**: Lunch close. Reviews wastage and remakes. Two remakes due to allergen confusion on the new dish.
- **16:00**: Files a feedback form to head office about the allergen layout on the spec sheet.
- **17:30**: Match build-up. The kitchen pivots to high-throughput match food.
- **19:00**: Match starts. He is calling the pass. KDS is at full load. One screen flickers but recovers.
- **22:00**: Match over. Late food kicks in. The kitchen cope.
- **00:30**: Service close. Walks the line. Runs the wastage and stock counts.
- **02:00**: Hands over to the night cleaning shift. Goes home.

---

## Goals

- Deliver the central menu at spec, at throughput, on GP, every shift.
- Lead a kitchen that is top quartile on every audit line.
- Develop a sous chef into a head chef role within 18 months.
- Move into a Group Executive Chef pipeline within five years.
- Zero allergen incidents.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- The central menu sometimes ships dishes that work in a test kitchen and fail in a venue under Saturday load.
- KDS handling of modifiers and allergens is centrally configured. He cannot tweak it.
- Stock and ordering are centrally controlled. When the central system gets a supplier swap wrong, his kitchen wears the consequence.
- His feedback to central takes weeks to result in any change.
- Wastage reporting is delayed and at a level of granularity that hides the answer.
- A KDS flicker during a peak service is a one-in-five chance of a missed order. The group's tolerance for this is implicitly higher than his.

---

## KPIs he is judged on

- Food GP% versus venue plan.
- Wastage as a percentage of food cost.
- Remake and comp rate.
- Allergen incident count.
- Audit score (food safety, kitchen cleanliness, brand standards).
- Staff retention and training completion.

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in his hand**: KDS screens on multiple stations, a back-office PC, his corporate phone, the venue tablet for audits and forms.
- **Current stack**: Centrally configured KDS, central menu and recipe system, central stock and ordering, audit app, comms platform.
- **Comfort level**: Functional. Fluent in what he is given. Will not tinker.
- **What he will not tolerate**: A KDS that drops a ticket. A modifier or allergen that does not arrive as it left.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Kitchen operational decisions inside venue. Kitchen hires within budget. Local prep adjustments within central spec.
- **Influences**: His sous chef development. His audit score. Indirectly, central menu through feedback loops.
- **Cannot touch**: Menu, recipe, supplier list, KDS configuration, food cost pricing.
- **Who he needs on side**: The GM. The Group Executive Chef (for any central change). His sous chefs.

---

## Quotes

> "Spec is spec. I deliver spec. The system has to deliver spec back to me on the screen."

> "If the modifier comes through wrong, I have an allergen risk. Nothing else matters in that moment."

> "Central menu launches that have not been tested at 2,000 covers a day are central menu launches I will be fixing in venue."

---

## What he needs from Oolio

- A KDS that is rock solid, especially on modifiers and allergens, especially at peak.
- Sub-venue granularity on wastage and stock reporting.
- A faster loop between his feedback and central product changes.
- Real-world stress testing on the platform at venues like his, before features ship.
- Audit and food safety integrations that reduce his admin tail.

---

## What loses him

- A KDS update that loses an order.
- A modifier handling bug.
- A central change rolled out without venue-scale testing.
- A response time longer than minutes during peak.

---

## Strategic signal

Andrew is the kitchen voice in the enterprise segment. The COO will not approve a platform that fails the kitchen, because the kitchen failure cascades into allergen, food safety, and brand risk. He is also the persona who keeps Oolio honest about whether the KDS is enterprise-grade or just enterprise-priced. The group's reference visits include his kitchen. His sign-off is part of the deal.
