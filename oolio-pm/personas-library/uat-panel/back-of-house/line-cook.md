# Ahmed Khalil

> On the line, eyes on the KDS, doesn't look up for four hours.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Chef de Partie (CDP) on the grill section
- **Age**: 27
- **Location**: Melbourne, Australia
- **Venue**: A 140-seat casual dining restaurant. Independent, part of a small group of three venues.
- **Reports to**: The sous chef and the head chef.
- **Manages**: A commis chef during peak.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Small group, 1 of 3 venues
- **Service style**: Casual dining
- **Geography**: Australia, Melbourne
- **Tech maturity**: Reactive
- **Buying motion**: Not a buyer. The persona who has to live with the system the head chef and GM picked.

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: ordermate or the OOLIO platform with a KDS at his station.
- **Solutions they touch**: KDS (constantly), nothing else.

---

## Bio

Ahmed trained in a hotel kitchen for three years and has been on a restaurant line for the last four. He is fast, calm, accurate. He is the kind of cook the sous chef puts on the grill on a Saturday and stops worrying about.

He cares about clean tickets, a clear pass, and a sous chef who can call a service. He does not care what brand of KDS is on the wall. He cares that it is on. He cares that the tickets are right. He cares that nobody is shouting because the system has lost an order.

He is ambitious in a quiet way. He wants to be a sous chef in two years and a head chef in five.

---

## A day in the life

A Friday.

- **15:00**: Arrives. Sets up his section. Sharpens. Mises en place.
- **17:00**: Family meal. Pre-service brief.
- **18:00**: Service starts. KDS is on. Tickets flow.
- **19:30**: Peak. He has 11 tickets up. The KDS turns red on the oldest one. He calls the sous. They reorganise.
- **20:30**: An allergen ticket comes through. He stops, reads the modifier, calls it across the pass. Confirms with the sous. Plates it separately.
- **21:30**: KDS flickers and one ticket disappears for three seconds before reappearing. He calls it. The pass confirms it is still in the system.
- **23:30**: Service slows. Cleans down. KDS goes into close mode.
- **00:30**: Goes home.

---

## Goals

- Get through service cleanly.
- Not be the reason a ticket gets remade.
- Become a sous chef.
- Be the person the head chef trusts on a Saturday.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- A KDS screen that flickers, lags, or visibly drops a ticket, even for a moment.
- Modifier and allergen information that is not visually distinct from normal tickets.
- A bump-back from the floor that arrives after the dish has been plated.
- A screen that is poorly mounted, too small, too dim, or in the wrong place.
- New menu items that go live without him being trained on the plating.

---

## KPIs he is judged on

- Implicitly: speed, accuracy, calm under pressure, the sous chef's trust.
- Formally: nothing.

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in his hand**: A KDS screen and a bump bar. That is it.
- **Current stack**: The KDS the venue runs. He has no input.
- **Comfort level**: Reactive. He learns the screen on his first shift. He will not learn it twice.
- **What he will not tolerate**: A KDS that drops a ticket. A modifier that is not clear.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Nothing.
- **Influences**: The sous chef's confidence in the KDS. If he says it is unreliable, the kitchen treats it as unreliable.
- **Cannot touch**: Everything outside his station.
- **Who he needs on side**: The sous chef.

---

## Quotes

> "If the ticket is on the screen, I will cook it. If the screen blinks, I don't trust it."

> "Tell me what is in the modifier. Make it obvious. I do not have time to read paragraphs."

> "Show me the allergen at the top of the ticket in red. Not buried."

---

## What he needs from Oolio

- A KDS that is rock solid. Never blinks. Never drops a ticket.
- Modifiers and allergens that are visually unmistakable.
- A bump flow that takes one bump.
- A screen and bump bar designed for hands that are wet, hot, and busy.
- A 60-second on-screen brief when a new menu item drops.

---

## What loses him

- A KDS that visibly fails once.
- A modifier hidden in body text.
- A bump bar that needs two presses.
- A screen too small to read from his station.

---

## Strategic signal

Ahmed is the persona that exposes the gap between "the KDS works" and "the KDS works at 9pm on a Saturday on the grill section in a hot kitchen with eleven tickets up". Investing in KDS hardware design, ticket layout, modifier rendering, and bump-bar ergonomics is investing in him. Every order through Oolio's platform passes through someone like him. He is the second-highest-volume persona in the library, after the bartender, and the second most often forgotten.
