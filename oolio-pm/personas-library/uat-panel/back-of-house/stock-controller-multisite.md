# Helena Mendes

> Reconciles 12 venues, two warehouses, and 84 suppliers. Every week.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Group Stock and Purchasing Manager
- **Age**: 39
- **Location**: Sydney, Australia, head office above one of the group's venues
- **Venue**: Twelve venues in a small-to-mid market group. Mix of bars, restaurants, and pubs. Two central storerooms. 84 active suppliers.
- **Reports to**: The Group Operations Director, and dotted line to the Group CFO.
- **Manages**: A team of two stock controllers in head office and (informally) the venue-level staff who run the local counts.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Small group, edging into mid-market group, 12 venues
- **Service style**: Mixed
- **Geography**: Australia, Sydney
- **Tech maturity**: Deliberate
- **Buying motion**: Functional committee (with Operations Director and Finance)

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: Mixed POS (typical of a small group in transition). She is the persona pushing hardest for a single platform across the group.
- **Solutions they touch**: Stock and ordering, recipe costing, supplier integrations, BI and reporting, POS (for sales data, read-only).

---

## Bio

Helena did a commerce degree, worked in retail FMCG procurement for six years, moved into hospitality at 30 because she wanted to work somewhere where her decisions changed something visible by Saturday. She has been at this group for four years.

She lives in spreadsheets, supplier portals, and a stock tool that is fine for one venue and bad for twelve. Every Monday morning she runs the variance report. Every Monday afternoon she has six conversations about it. Every Tuesday she reorders.

She is one of the few people in the group who sees every venue. She knows where the leaks are. She has opinions about which venues are well run and which are not. The owners listen to her, sometimes.

---

## A day in the life

A Monday.

- **08:00**: Coffee at her desk. Pulls the weekend's sales by venue from the POS extracts.
- **09:00**: Variance meeting with the Ops Director. Two venues are outside tolerance.
- **10:30**: Calls the GM of the venue most outside tolerance. Asks specific questions about specific bottles.
- **12:00**: Reorders across all 12 venues. Three supplier portals. One supplier still takes a phone order.
- **14:00**: Reviews a recipe costing project the head chefs are jointly running. Three of the menus are now in the central recipe tool. Nine are not.
- **15:30**: Calls a wine importer to renegotiate a price line.
- **17:00**: Builds the weekly variance report for the Ops Director. The build takes 45 minutes because the data comes from three places.
- **18:30**: Goes home.

---

## Goals

- A single stock and ordering platform across the group, fed by a single POS.
- Group variance below 1%.
- Supplier consolidation that takes the active supplier list from 84 to 50 within 18 months.
- A weekly variance report that builds itself.
- Get to a place where she is steering the group's stock strategy, not chasing it.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- The mix of POS systems means stock data is reconciled by spreadsheet.
- Recipe costing relies on head chefs entering recipes correctly into a central tool. Some do, some don't.
- Supplier prices change without warning. Margins are eaten before she sees the change.
- Variance reporting tells her there is a problem. It does not tell her where.
- Venue-level stock counts are inconsistent. Some are accurate, some are guesses.
- She has 84 suppliers and at least 12 of them are still phone or email orders.

---

## KPIs she is judged on

- Group stock variance.
- Cost of goods sold per category per venue (versus benchmarks).
- Days of stock held.
- Supplier consolidation progress.
- Successful delivery of any stock-related platform projects.

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in her hand**: A laptop almost permanently. An iPad on site visits. Her phone.
- **Current stack**: A stock tool (one of two depending on venue), POS extracts, supplier portals, Xero, a heavy Google Sheets workbook, email.
- **Comfort level**: Deliberate. She knows what good looks like. She has lived with bad for years.
- **What she will not tolerate**: A stock tool that does not reconcile with the POS. A variance report that disagrees with itself between two runs.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Stock and supplier decisions within the agreed framework. Reorders. Supplier renegotiations within margin tolerances.
- **Influences**: POS choice (heavily, on the stock side). Stock tool choice. Recipe tool choice.
- **Cannot touch**: Menu, pricing, capex.
- **Who she needs on side**: The Ops Director, the CFO, the head chefs, the GMs.

---

## Quotes

> "When the price of beef goes up on a Wednesday, I should know by Wednesday, not by month end."

---

## What she needs from Oolio

- One stock and ordering platform across the group, fed by one POS, that reconciles to the cent.
- A recipe costing module that updates from real supplier prices on real schedules.
- A variance report that points to a likely cause.
- Supplier integrations for at least the top 30 suppliers in her stack.
- A view of cross-venue benchmarks. She wants to know how venue 7 compares to venue 3 on lamb cost, not just her own group's average.

---

## What loses her

- A stock report that disagrees with the POS extract.
- A recipe module that does not update.
- A roadmap that puts stock and BOH features at the back of the queue.
- A salesperson who pitches the loyalty product first.

---

## Strategic signal

Helena is the persona who proves Oolio's "platform" claim in the small-to-mid group segment. If Oolio can give her one platform that reconciles, the rest of the group goes with it, because the financial control argument she will make to the owner and the CFO is unanswerable. She is also the single biggest believer in the consultancy proposition, because she has lived without it. Helena will pay for benchmarking, supplier intelligence, and operational advice on top of the platform. She is a willing buyer of the next layer of value Oolio is building.
