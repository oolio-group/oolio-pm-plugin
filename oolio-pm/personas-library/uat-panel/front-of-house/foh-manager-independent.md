# Bella Ricci

> Owns the floor. Knows every regular's drink.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Front of House Manager, Marlow and Co
- **Age**: 29
- **Location**: Auckland, New Zealand
- **Venue**: Marlow and Co. A 70-seat neighbourhood bistro with a 14-seat bar. Owner-operated. Dinner six nights, brunch on weekends.
- **Reports to**: The owner-operator (who is in the kitchen most nights).
- **Manages**: A team of nine: two senior servers, four servers, a host, a runner, a junior bartender.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Independent, 1 venue
- **Service style**: Casual dining restaurant
- **Geography**: New Zealand, Auckland
- **Tech maturity**: Functional, trending Deliberate
- **Buying motion**: Owner-led, with strong FOH influence

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: idealpos or ordermate
- **Solutions they touch**: POS, table management, payments, reservations integration, basic reporting, tip pooling

---

## Bio

Bella has worked floor for ten years. She came up through busy cafes and a hotel restaurant before landing at Marlow and Co four years ago, where she has been FOH manager for the last two. She is the second-in-command at the venue. The owner runs the kitchen and the books. Bella runs everything that the guest touches.

She is the one who knows the regulars. She is also the one who decides which staff member is on which section. She holds the room together and most nights the guest never knows she exists, which is the point.

She is paid a flat salary plus a share of the tip pool. Her ambition is to move into a more senior role at a more ambitious venue, or to open her own small wine bar one day.

---

## A day in the life

A Friday.

- **14:00**: Arrives. Goes through the reservation book. 84 booked, including a 12-top at 19:30.
- **14:30**: Builds the floor plan. Walks the team through it. Allocates sections.
- **15:30**: Walks the bar with the junior bartender. Checks the back bar against the wine list. One missing bottle from last night's count.
- **16:30**: Pre-service: menu changes briefing, allergens, the 12-top's notes.
- **17:30**: Doors open. Early sitting.
- **19:00**: Service in full. The 12-top wants their bill split twelve ways. The POS handles it slowly. She splits it manually.
- **20:30**: A regular walks in without a booking. She finds them seats at the bar. Comps them a glass.
- **22:00**: Last seating. She is on the door, then runs food, then back behind the bar to cover a break.
- **23:30**: Service close. Walks the floor with the senior servers. Resets for tomorrow.
- **00:30**: Cashes up. Closes the EOD on the POS. Sends a one-line summary to the owner.

---

## Goals

- A consistent guest experience every night.
- A floor team that runs without her constantly intervening.
- A floor where her senior staff are growing into her role.
- A clear path to her own venue or to a senior role at a destination restaurant.
- Tips above the city median, so the team stays.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- The POS works but is slow on splits, transfers, and complex tabs. These are the moments that matter most in service.
- The reservations system and the POS do not share guest data. She knows the regulars. The system does not.
- Tip pooling calculations are partly automated and partly her doing maths on the back office PC at 1am.
- Onboarding a new server on the POS takes more shifts than she would like.
- Loyalty is a tray of paper cards behind the bar.
- The owner asks her for views of the data she cannot easily produce.

---

## KPIs she is judged on

- Covers and average spend versus forecast.
- Guest feedback (Google, return-visit rate as far as she can see it).
- Staff retention.
- Tip pool size.
- The owner's mood on a Monday morning.

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in her hand**: POS terminals on the floor and at the bar, her phone, a reservations tablet at the host station, a back-office PC for end-of-day.
- **Current stack**: POS in the Oolio family, integrated payments terminal, a reservations platform (separate), Deputy for rostering, a WhatsApp group with the team, a Google Sheet for the wine list count.
- **Comfort level**: Functional. She uses what works. She has opinions on what does not.
- **What she will not tolerate**: A POS that slows down the moment of payment. Surprise updates. Anything that pulls her away from a guest.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Operational decisions within the venue. Casual hires. Section allocations. Daily ordering.
- **Influences**: POS choice (heavily). Reservations choice. Loyalty choice. The owner will defer to her on FOH tools.
- **Cannot touch**: Capital decisions. Menu and pricing.
- **Who she needs on side**: The owner. The two senior servers. The kitchen.

---

## Quotes

> "I do not need ten ways to take a payment. I need one way that always works."

> "The system should know our regulars. We do. It does not."

> "If I have to spend Sunday morning fixing the EOD, the system has lost."

---

## What she needs from Oolio

- Fast splits, fast transfers, fast tab merges. The hard moments handled cleanly.
- One guest profile shared across reservations, POS, payments, loyalty.
- A tip pool calculation she trusts and the team trust.
- A training package that gets a new server productive on the till in two shifts.
- A back-office that closes the day without making her do arithmetic.

---

## What loses her

- A POS that hangs during payment.
- A reservation system that does not share data with the POS.
- A loyalty programme that asks a regular to sign up at the table.
- An onboarding flow that takes her off the floor.

---

## Strategic signal

Bella is the daily user persona for the independent fine-casual segment. She is also the talent that becomes Sam (independent GM) and then becomes Mel (independent owner) over time. The library treats her as a multi-stage relationship, not a single role. If Oolio's tools respect the floor, she will carry Oolio with her for 20 years of her career.
