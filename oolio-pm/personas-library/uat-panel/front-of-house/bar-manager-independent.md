# Tomás "Tom" Lavelle

> Runs the bar like a kitchen. Margins to two decimal places.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Bar Manager, The Glass House
- **Age**: 32
- **Location**: Dublin, Ireland
- **Venue**: The Glass House. A 60-cover cocktail and natural wine bar. Late trade, no food beyond bar snacks. Independent, owner is involved but not on the floor.
- **Reports to**: The owner.
- **Manages**: A team of six bartenders and two bar backs.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Independent, 1 venue
- **Service style**: Bar (independent)
- **Geography**: Ireland, Dublin
- **Tech maturity**: Deliberate
- **Buying motion**: Owner-led, with strong bar manager influence

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: ordermate or idealpos
- **Solutions they touch**: POS, payments, stock, recipe costing, basic loyalty, integrations with stock and accounting

---

## Bio

Tom is a competition bartender who moved into management at 28. He has worked four bars in Dublin and one in London. He treats a cocktail bar like a kitchen: recipes, costing, pours measured to the millilitre, stock taken weekly without exception. He is the reason the bar's GP% is two points above the local average.

He is curious about technology in the way a craftsperson is curious about better tools. He will read a release note. He will also write a one-page brief on why a particular feature is bad for the bar.

He is not interested in becoming a GM. He is interested in opening his own bar, which would be partly funded by the owner of The Glass House, who has hinted at it.

---

## A day in the life

A Thursday.

- **14:00**: Arrives. Receives a wine delivery. Checks it line by line against the order.
- **15:00**: Updates the cocktail menu in the POS. A new spec was finalised yesterday. Adds the recipe to the stock tool so the pour is costed and depleted automatically.
- **16:30**: Pre-service brief. Walks the team through three new cocktails and a wine he wants pushed. Tastes them with the team.
- **18:00**: Doors open.
- **20:30**: Bar full. Two of the team are slower than he would like on the new cocktails. He steps in.
- **22:00**: A regular orders a round and pays by card. The payment terminal hangs for 30 seconds. He apologises. The terminal is intermittent on Thursdays for reasons he and his support contact cannot pin down.
- **00:30**: Service ends. He runs the stock view in the POS to see which lines moved.
- **01:30**: Locks up.

---

## Goals

- Maintain GP% at least two points above the local market.
- Run a bar where staff want to stay and where their craft is taken seriously.
- Build a wine programme that gets the bar written about.
- Open his own bar, with the owner backing him, within three years.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- Recipe costing in the POS is set up but not always trusted, because pours drift.
- Stock variance is a recurring conversation. He can see it. He cannot always explain it.
- Payments terminal reliability is inconsistent.
- Loyalty in his world is recognition, not points. The off-the-shelf loyalty tool is the wrong shape.
- Reporting on cocktail-by-cocktail margin is something he builds himself in a spreadsheet.
- He has to type the same supplier price changes into two systems.

---

## KPIs he is judged on

- GP% on the bar.
- Stock variance against theoretical (weekly).
- Cover-to-revenue ratio.
- Staff retention.
- Media mentions and industry recognition (informal but the owner cares).

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in his hand**: POS terminals at the bar, an iPad in the back office for stock and ordering, his phone, the payment terminal.
- **Current stack**: POS in the Oolio family, integrated payments, a stock tool, a separate ordering tool for wine suppliers, Xero (read-only access), a Google Sheet for the cocktail recipe ledger, an Instagram account for the bar.
- **Comfort level**: Deliberate. Wants the right tool. Will tolerate complexity if it earns him something.
- **What he will not tolerate**: A payment terminal that drops mid-transaction with a regular. An inaccurate stock count caused by the system.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Bar-related operational decisions. Supplier choice within the agreed list. Casual bar hires.
- **Influences**: POS and payments choice. Stock tool choice. The owner will defer to him on bar tools.
- **Cannot touch**: Capital. Lease. Brand identity.
- **Who he needs on side**: The owner. His senior bartender. The FOH manager (if there is one; sometimes Tom is acting FOH).

---

## Quotes

> "Stock variance is not a mystery. It is either pours, theft, or the system. The system should never be the answer."

> "If your loyalty product asks my regular to scan a QR code, you have just told my regular they are not a regular."

> "I will read your release notes. Stop hiding the important changes in them."

---

## What he needs from Oolio

- A recipe-and-pour costing model that he trusts.
- Stock variance reporting that points to a likely cause, not a number.
- A payments terminal that is boring. He wants boring.
- A way to recognise regulars without making them complete a form.
- A direct line to a product manager who understands bar craft.

---

## What loses him

- A payments terminal that hangs once a week.
- A loyalty product designed for cafes.
- A POS update that changes the cocktail order flow without warning.
- A stock report that does not reconcile.

---

## Strategic signal

Tom is the persona who tests Oolio's credibility with craft-led independent venues. These are the venues that punch above their weight in industry voice. Winning Tom is winning a media multiplier, not just a venue. He is also a future founder. The bar he opens in three years is the same Mel-shaped persona at year zero, except he is even more opinionated.
