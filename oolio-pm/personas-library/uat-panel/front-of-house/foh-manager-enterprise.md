# Priya Sharma

> Deputy GM in everything but title. Holds up the floor for an estate.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Front of House Manager (Deputy GM grade), at a 320-cover enterprise pub
- **Age**: 31
- **Location**: Manchester, United Kingdom
- **Venue**: A flagship venue inside the same ~620-site UK pub group as Liam Henderson. City centre, multi-floor, food and bar trade, gaming corner, 12 letting rooms.
- **Reports to**: The venue GM.
- **Manages**: A team of ~30 FOH staff in two shifts. Shift leaders, bar staff, food runners, hosts.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Enterprise chain, 1 of 620 venues
- **Service style**: Pub and hotel (multi-revenue)
- **Geography**: United Kingdom, Manchester
- **Tech maturity**: Functional
- **Buying motion**: Procurement-led (she is a user, not a buyer)

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: bepoz at venue level. Chosen by head office.
- **Solutions they touch**: POS, payments, KDS, loyalty (centrally controlled), labour scheduling, audit.

---

## Bio

Priya started as a bar staff member at university in Leeds, kept it as her job after graduating, moved into a shift leader role at 25, an FOH manager role at 28. This is her second venue at FOH manager grade. She is on the GM development pathway, six to twelve months out from her first GM role.

She is what head office calls "ready". The thing standing between her and a GM role is not skill, it is a venue becoming available. In the meantime, she runs everything below the GM line.

She is loyal to the brand. She is also frank about what works and what does not. She is the kind of FOH manager that Area Operations Managers quietly call when they want to know what is really happening on the ground.

---

## A day in the life

A Saturday with a match on.

- **10:30**: Arrives. Reviews the day's plan. 480 covers forecast in the dining area, plus standing trade for the football.
- **11:00**: Walks the floors with the shift leaders. Sets sections. Briefs on the new menu items.
- **12:00**: Lunch opens. She is on the bar for the first hour to feel the flow.
- **14:30**: Match kicks off in two hours. She converts a section of the floor into a standing area. The POS configuration for the standing area was set up by head office two months ago and tested once. It mostly works.
- **16:30**: Match kicks off. Bar packed five deep. Two POS terminals are slow on round throughput. She moves a senior bar staff member onto a third terminal and reorganises the queue.
- **18:30**: Half time. 90 seconds of chaos at the bar. She watches three terminals to see which is keeping up.
- **20:30**: Match over. Crowd thins. She rotates staff onto breaks.
- **22:30**: Late food service. The kitchen flags KDS hanging on a complex modifier.
- **00:00**: Service close. She runs the late audit. Submits.
- **01:00**: Drives home.

---

## Goals

- Run a clean shift, top quartile on every audit line.
- Be promoted to GM in the next 12 months.
- Build a shift leader bench that can run the floor without her on a quiet weeknight.
- Hit the bar throughput numbers head office set for match days.
- Not lose a Saturday to a tech incident.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- The POS workflow for round throughput at peak (a five-deep bar) is the difference between a great day and a complaint. Every extra click matters.
- KDS modifier handling is fragile on the new menu items. The kitchen blames the system. Sometimes they are right.
- Labour scheduling is centralised and the forecast does not always match the venue's real shape. She edits it weekly.
- Loyalty sign-ups in venue are minimal because the in-venue flow is clunky.
- Audit app, comms app, POS, labour, KDS are separate logins on the back office PC.
- She has good ideas about how the venue should work. The route to head office is long.

---

## KPIs she is judged on

- Bar throughput per hour during peak (a head-office metric).
- Audit score weekly.
- Customer feedback weekly.
- Staff retention and training completion.
- Wage% versus venue plan.
- Loyalty sign-ups in venue (recently added, low priority).

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in her hand**: Corporate Android phone, POS terminals at the bar and floor, a back-office PC, an audit tablet.
- **Current stack**: Enterprise POS, enterprise KDS, head office labour tool, audit app, comms platform, central loyalty.
- **Comfort level**: Functional. She can spot a workflow problem in five seconds. She does not need to understand the API.
- **What she will not tolerate**: A peak-hour slowdown. A poorly-tested update. A KDS that drops orders silently.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Operational decisions on her shift. Casual hires in agreement with GM. Local section configuration within head office framework.
- **Influences**: Venue-level operational changes. Shift leader promotions.
- **Cannot touch**: POS, payments, supplier list, menu, pricing, brand standards.
- **Who she needs on side**: The GM. Her shift leaders. The kitchen.

---

## Quotes

> "Three clicks on a round at half-time is not three clicks. It is a hundred unhappy people."

> "If you tested the new menu on a Tuesday afternoon, you did not test the new menu."

> "I would tell head office what is wrong. There is no obvious way to do that other than my GM telling his Area Ops Manager."

---

## What she needs from Oolio

- A POS workflow for high-throughput bar moments that has been designed for high-throughput bar moments. Not adapted to them.
- A KDS that handles modifiers reliably.
- A clear, fast loyalty sign-up flow at the bar that takes less than ten seconds.
- A way for venue feedback on tech to actually reach the team that builds it.
- Training material that respects the time her team have.

---

## What loses her

- A peak-hour outage with a slow response.
- A new feature shipped without a real venue test.
- A KDS that silently drops orders.
- Loyalty that interrupts service.

---

## Strategic signal

Priya is the FOH user experience in the enterprise segment. The COO buys the platform, the GM validates it, Priya is the persona whose shift the platform either succeeds or fails on. Her experience is the daily evidence. If Oolio invests in enterprise without investing in Priya's workflow, the platform will lose its enterprise pilots in week three.
