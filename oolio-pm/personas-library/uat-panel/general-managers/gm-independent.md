# Sam Patel

> Holds the room together. Five nights a week.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: General Manager, Lantern Row
- **Age**: 34
- **Location**: Sydney, Australia
- **Venue**: Lantern Row. 92-seat modern Australian restaurant in Surry Hills. Two services a day, six days a week. Average cover $95.
- **Reports to**: The owner (an absentee chef who runs another venue and owns 60% of this one).
- **Manages**: 22 FOH staff including two senior section heads, a sommelier, a host, runners, and a small bar team.

### Segmentation

- **Size and ownership**: Independent, 1 venue
- **Service style**: Fine dining restaurant
- **Geography**: Australia, Sydney
- **Tech maturity**: Deliberate
- **Buying motion**: Owner-led, with strong GM influence

### Oolio fit

- **Brand they would be on today**: ordermate (table management, at-table ordering, service-led venue)
- **Solutions they touch**: POS, table management, KDS, payments, reservations integration, loyalty (light), reporting

---

## Bio

Sam trained as a chef briefly, decided service was where the energy was, and moved into FOH at 22. Eight years in three serious restaurants, including two with hats. Took the GM role at Lantern Row 18 months ago. The brief was to lift the room from "good" to "destination", and to get the venue commercially healthy enough that the owner stops worrying about it.

He runs a tight floor. He knows every regular by name and most by drink. He thinks of the POS as part of his service, not separate from it. A slow till is a slow night.

He is ambitious. He sees this role as the step before opening his own. He is paying attention to how the owner runs the business, not just the venue.

---

## A day in the life

A Saturday.

- **10:00**: In the venue. Checks the reservation book against the table plan. Three changes already needed.
- **11:00**: Pre-service briefing with FOH. Reads the menu changes. Sets the section heads. Walks through allergens.
- **12:00**: Lunch service. He is on the door, then on the floor, then on the pass.
- **15:30**: Lunch close. Debrief. Counts cash. Looks at lunch covers versus forecast.
- **16:30**: Bar restock. Discovers two bottles of gin are missing from the bar count. Notes it. Will speak to the bartender quietly tomorrow.
- **17:30**: Pre-dinner briefing. 96 booked. Three walk-in tables to hold for regulars.
- **19:30**: Dinner peak. A VIP arrives unbooked. He finds them a table by moving two parties. The POS reroutes their tab from one section to another. It works. Most of the time.
- **22:00**: Service winding down. Reviews the night's covers, average spend, and tip pool on the POS.
- **23:30**: Last guests out. Sits with a glass of wine at the bar. Replies to a text from the owner who is asking about Friday's numbers.

---

## Goals

- A consistently great service. Every night. Not just the good ones.
- Hit the cover and average-spend targets the owner sets.
- Develop two of his senior staff into floor manager roles, so he is not the single point of failure.
- Be on the path to opening his own venue within three years.
- Make the owner trust him enough to leave him alone.

---

## Frustrations and pain points

- Reservation system and POS are not the same product. He has to reconcile mentally.
- Table moves and tab transfers during service work, but with friction. Each click costs the table experience.
- Loyalty data is captured but he does not feel he can use it. He cannot easily see "this guest's last three visits".
- Reporting tells him what happened. It does not tell him what to do tomorrow.
- The owner asks him for numbers in formats the POS does not produce. He builds them by hand.
- Training a new senior server on the POS is a three-shift job and they are still slow on it after a month.

---

## KPIs he is judged on

- Weekly covers and weekly revenue against forecast.
- Average spend per cover.
- Wage cost as a percentage of revenue.
- Guest feedback scores (Google, OpenTable, in-house).
- Staff retention (informal, but the owner notices).

---

## Tech profile

- **Devices in his hand**: A POS terminal he carries during service, an iPad for floor management, his iPhone, a reservations tablet at the door.
- **Current stack**: ordermate (POS), a reservations platform, a payments terminal integrated with the POS, a rostering app, Slack, the owner's WhatsApp.
- **Comfort level**: Deliberate. Will master a tool he respects. Will resent a tool that wastes service time.
- **What he will not tolerate**: Anything that slows a table down. Surprise charges to his stripe of the business. Software that updates without telling him on a Friday.

---

## Decision-making power

- **Can sign for**: Operational decisions inside venue (supplier choice within agreed framework, hires up to senior server level, weekly purchase orders, marketing within owner-set budget).
- **Influences**: POS, payments, reservations, loyalty, training tools. The owner will not change any of these without Sam's sign-off.
- **Cannot touch**: Capex, lease, head chef compensation, anything legal.
- **Who he needs on side**: The owner (decision power), the head chef (operational alignment), his senior section heads (delivery).

---

## Quotes

> "Service is a hundred small moments. The till is in about thirty of them. If the till is good, I have a chance. If it is bad, I do not."

> "I do not want more reports. I want one report that tells me whether tonight is going to be different from last Saturday."

> "I will pick the system. The owner pays for it, but he will pick what I pick."

---

## What he needs from Oolio

- A POS that disappears during service. Fast. Reliable. No drama.
- A guest view that lets him serve a regular like a regular.
- One trusted weekly view that he can send to the owner without rebuilding it.
- Honest training material that is shorter than the actual training he has to do.
- A relationship with someone at Oolio who actually understands service, not just software.

---

## What loses him

- A POS that drops the table when a tab is transferred.
- An update pushed on a Friday afternoon.
- A salesperson who pitches features instead of asking about service.
- Loyalty that asks the guest to download an app at the table.

---

## Strategic signal

Sam is the GM persona that proves the consultancy proposition at the independent end. He will switch systems if the new one helps him run a better room. He will champion Oolio internally and to peers if it does. He is also the future small-group owner. Win him as a GM today, win him as an owner in five years. This is a long-term acquisition persona, not just an operational one.
