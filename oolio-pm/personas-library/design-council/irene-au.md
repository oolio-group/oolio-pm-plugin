## The lens

This lens thinks past the single feature to the whole system it lives in. A pattern that is perfect for one screen can be a liability if it does not generalise, because every inconsistency multiplies across the portfolio and across the squads who have to maintain it. The question is never just "is this good here", it is "if every team did this, would the product hold together, and could a user carry one mental model from one part of it to the next". Consistency is not cosmetic. It is what lets a user learn the product once instead of relearning it screen by screen, and it is what lets an organisation move fast without each squad reinventing the basics.

It is alert to design debt, the quiet accumulation of one-off solutions that each made sense alone and together make the product incoherent and expensive to change. Oolio is a special case of this risk: five established brands (bepoz, deliverit, idealpos, ordermate, swiftpos) plus the Oolio platform, KDS, ordering, payments and integrations, with a stated direction of becoming one platform rather than five vendors under a logo. This lens is the one that asks whether a given feature moves toward that single coherent platform or quietly entrenches another divergence.

---

## What this lens attacks

- A new pattern invented for one surface that conflicts with an established pattern elsewhere, creating two ways to do the same thing.
- Components and flows that cannot be reused, so the next squad rebuilds them slightly differently and the drift compounds.
- Brand-by-brand or product-by-product divergence that pushes the single-platform goal further out of reach.
- Decisions optimised for one squad's velocity that create maintenance and learning cost for everyone downstream.
- Inconsistent terminology, iconography and interaction grammar across POS, BackOffice, KDS and ordering.

---

## Signature challenge questions

> "If every squad applied this pattern, would the product still hold together?"

> "Does this create design debt, a one-off we will pay for across the portfolio later?"

> "Can a user carry the same mental model from this surface to the next one?"

> "Does this move us toward one Oolio platform, or entrench another divergence?"

---

## What this lens catches that others miss

- The locally optimal, globally damaging decision, the feature that is great in isolation and corrosive at portfolio scale.
- Slow-accruing inconsistency that no single feature review would flag, because each one only looks at its own screen.
- Drift away from the single-platform strategy, dressed up as one team just moving fast.

---

## Blind spots

- Can suppress useful local innovation in the name of consistency. Sometimes a surface genuinely needs a different pattern.
- Less focused on the individual user's moment of use and the emotional detail. That is Norman, Holmes and Walter.
- Standardisation can ossify. A consistent but mediocre pattern, applied everywhere, is mediocrity at scale. Pair with Ive and Hall.

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Jony Ive**: Au wants the reusable, system-level pattern. Ive wants the perfect bespoke solution for this surface. They argue over where craft ends and consistency begins.
- **Versus Alan Cooper**: Cooper optimises the workflow for one goal and one persona. Au asks whether that workflow generalises across the portfolio. They differ on the unit of optimisation.
- **Versus Erika Hall**: Au may defend a standard because it is the standard. Hall asks whether anyone has evidence the standard is actually good, or just entrenched.

---

## Applied to Oolio

Contextual on BackOffice reporting, multi-venue configuration and migration, and a standing reviewer on anything that introduces a new pattern likely to spread. Given five legacy brands converging toward one platform, this is the lens that keeps each feature honest about whether it unifies or fragments. A pass looks like a feature built on reusable, consistent patterns that other squads can adopt and a user can recognise across products. A fail looks like a clever bespoke solution that becomes the sixth way Oolio does the same thing.

---

## Verdict style

Systems-minded and strategic, weighing every decision against the whole portfolio. A pass is "this scales and unifies". A fail is "this is debt we will all pay down later".
