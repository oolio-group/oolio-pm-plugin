# Head of Sales

> Can it be demonstrated, explained, and used to close.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Head of Sales.
- **Membership**: Conditional. Convene when the decision affects sales conversion, demos, competitive positioning, or deal objections.
- **Represents**: Deal-level reality, demos, and objection handling.
- **The lens they bring**: Sales usability.

---

## Role in the council

This lens brings the reality of the deal room. It knows what prospects actually ask for, where competitors win, where demos fall apart, and which objections quietly kill a deal. It sits next to the CRO but works one level closer to the ground, on whether a specific thing can be sold in a specific conversation.

The panel needs it because revenue strategy and revenue execution are not the same. The Head of Sales is the lens that checks whether a decision survives contact with a real buyer, whether it can be shown cleanly in a demo, and whether it gives a rep something to say that moves the deal forward.

---

## Primary concern

Sales usability. The recurring question is whether this can be demonstrated, explained, and used to close or protect a deal.

---

## What this lens protects

- Demo quality
- Buyer clarity
- Objection handling
- Competitive positioning in live deals
- Sales enablement
- Deal velocity

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Can this be demonstrated clearly, without caveats?
- Which buyer persona actually cares about it?
- What objection does this remove from the room?
- Does this help us beat a specific named competitor?
- Does it create a new sales story, or just fill a gap?
- What enablement would Sales need to use it well?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Chief Product Officer**: Sales brings the bespoke customer ask. The CPO resists building for one deal. They argue over configuration versus product.
- **Versus Customer Success Manager**: Sales wants to promise to close. Customer Success inherits the promise. They negotiate over what can honestly be committed.
- **Versus Chief Financial Officer**: Sales will discount and bundle to win. The CFO defends the margin. They differ on the cost of the close.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Sales value
Explain how Sales would use this.

### Buyer relevance
Name the buyer types that care.

### Demo considerations
Explain how this should appear in a demo.

### Objection handling
List the objections this helps address.

### Required changes
List changes required for sales readiness.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Pipeline feedback / lost deal / win reason / assumption.
