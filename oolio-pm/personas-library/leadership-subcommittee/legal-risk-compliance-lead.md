# Legal, Risk and Compliance Lead

> What obligation are we taking on, and where.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Legal, Risk and Compliance Lead.
- **Membership**: Conditional. Convene when the decision touches privacy, tax, payroll, payments, regulated reporting, contracts, or accessibility.
- **Represents**: Legal, regulatory, contractual, and compliance exposure.
- **The lens they bring**: Risk exposure.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether a product direction creates legal, regulatory, contractual, tax, privacy, employment, accessibility, or payment risk. It does not need to attend every review, but when a decision touches money, personal data, employment, payments, contracts, regulated reporting, or regional obligations, it must be in the room.

The panel needs it because obligations are cheap to take on and expensive to discover late. The Legal lens is the one that names what the business is committing to, which regional rules apply, and what must be reviewed or changed before a decision can safely move. It protects Oolio from making claims, or shipping behaviour, it cannot stand behind.

---

## Primary concern

Risk exposure. The recurring question is what legal, regulatory, or contractual obligation this creates, and whether it can be met.

---

## What this lens protects

- Privacy compliance
- Contractual obligations
- Tax and reporting rules
- Employment and payroll-related risk
- Payment-related obligations
- Accessibility obligations
- Regional compliance

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Does this process personal, financial, employment, or otherwise sensitive data?
- Are there regional legal differences we must handle?
- Does this affect tax, payments, payroll, or regulated reporting?
- Do contracts or terms need updating before this ships?
- Are the customer-facing claims legally safe?
- Is there accessibility risk that creates an obligation?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Marketing or GTM Lead**: Marketing wants the bold claim. Legal wants it defensible. They argue over what can be said in market.
- **Versus Chief Revenue Officer**: The CRO wants to commit to what closes the deal. Legal weighs what the contract can actually promise. They negotiate over speed versus exposure.
- **Versus CTO or Head of Engineering**: Compliance can require handling and controls that add engineering cost. They differ on what must be built to meet the obligation.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Compliance view
Explain the relevant compliance considerations.

### Legal risks
List the legal or contractual risks.

### Regional risks
List the market-specific obligations.

### Required reviews
List the legal, tax, privacy, or compliance reviews required.

### Required changes
List the product or documentation changes required.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Legal review / regulatory requirement / contract review / assumption.
