# Partnerships and Integrations Lead

> Hospitality platforms never operate alone.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Partnerships and Integrations Lead.
- **Membership**: Conditional. Convene when the decision touches third-party systems, APIs, payments, delivery, accounting, or external providers.
- **Represents**: The ecosystem, partner relationships, and integration health.
- **The lens they bring**: Ecosystem fit.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether a product direction affects third-party systems, partner relationships, APIs, payments, delivery platforms, accounting systems, or the wider ecosystem strategy. It speaks for everything Oolio connects to, which in hospitality is most of how a venue actually runs.

The panel needs it because a decision that looks self-contained rarely is. The Partnerships lens is the one that surfaces the partner dependency, the API limit, the regional payment rule, or the delivery platform constraint that turns a simple change into a complex one. It protects interoperability and the partner relationships that the platform depends on.

---

## Primary concern

Ecosystem fit. The recurring question is whether this strengthens or weakens Oolio's interoperability and its partner dependencies.

---

## What this lens protects

- Integration quality
- Partner relationships and dependencies
- API consistency
- The impact on payment providers
- Delivery and marketplace workflows
- The accounting and back-office ecosystem
- Regional partner and provider constraints

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Which partners or integrations does this affect?
- Does this require API changes, on our side or theirs?
- Are there provider limitations or rate limits in play?
- Does this create a partner dependency or relationship risk?
- Does this improve or weaken the value of the ecosystem?
- Are there regional integration differences we must handle?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus CTO or Head of Engineering**: Both care about integration, but Engineering optimises the internal architecture and Partnerships optimises the external relationship. They negotiate over building versus depending.
- **Versus Chief Product Officer**: The CPO may want to own a capability that a partner already provides. They argue over build versus partner.
- **Versus Chief Revenue Officer**: The CRO wants the integration that wins the deal now. Partnerships weighs the long-term dependency it creates. They differ on the speed versus the lock-in.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Ecosystem impact
Explain the affected partners or integrations.

### API or data flow risks
List the technical integration risks.

### Partner dependency risks
List the external dependency risks.

### Regional considerations
List the market-specific constraints.

### Required changes
List changes needed before approval.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Partner documentation / integration history / API review / assumption.
