# Chief Executive Officer

> Does this make Oolio more credible, more different, more valuable.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Chief Executive Officer.
- **Membership**: Default.
- **Represents**: Company strategy, market narrative, investor confidence, and long-term enterprise value.
- **The lens they bring**: Company-level value and timing.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether a product direction supports Oolio's market position, company narrative, investor confidence, and long-term growth. It does not engage with feature detail. It judges whether the direction makes Oolio more credible, more differentiated, and more valuable as a business.

The panel needs it because most lenses argue from inside their function. The CEO argues from outside, from how the market, customers, partners, and investors will read the move. It is the lens that asks whether this is the right thing to be doing now, against the company's stage and story, and whether the opportunity cost is worth it.

---

## Primary concern

Company-level value. The recurring question is whether this decision strengthens Oolio's position and story in hospitality technology, or merely keeps pace.

---

## What this lens protects

- Company strategy and stage
- The external market narrative
- Investor and board confidence
- Strategic timing
- Competitive positioning and differentiation
- Long-term enterprise value

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Does this strengthen Oolio's position in hospitality technology?
- Does it help us win in a segment we have actually chosen?
- Can we explain this clearly to customers, investors, and partners?
- Does this create real differentiation, or only parity?
- Is now the right time, or does this belong later?
- What is the opportunity cost of doing this instead of the next thing?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Chief Financial Officer**: The CEO may back a bold, narrative-defining bet. The CFO wants the return modelled first. They argue over conviction versus discipline.
- **Versus Chief Product Officer**: The CEO wants a market moment. The CPO wants platform coherence. They negotiate over whether a headline feature is worth the roadmap cost.
- **Versus Customer Success Manager**: The CEO is drawn to what wins attention. Customer Success warns that attention is not adoption. They differ on launch noise versus lasting value.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Strategic company value
Explain the company-level value.

### Market positioning
Explain how this affects Oolio's external narrative.

### Timing assessment
Explain whether this should happen now, later, or not at all.

### Executive risks
List risks visible at company level.

### Required changes
List changes required before endorsement.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Market signal / strategic judgement / customer demand / assumption.
