# Customer Success Manager

> Sold value and adopted value are not the same thing.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Customer Success Manager.
- **Membership**: Default.
- **Represents**: Adoption, retention, expansion, and account health after go-live.
- **The lens they bring**: Customer value after go-live.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether customers will actually adopt, understand, and keep using a product after launch. It speaks for what happens once the sale is closed and the demo is over, when the value has to be real enough that a busy venue changes its behaviour to use it.

The panel needs it because the commercial lenses optimise for the sale, and the sale is not where value is realised. The Customer Success lens is the one that separates what was sold from what gets adopted, and asks whether the feature gives customers a reason to stay, expand, and renew, or whether it will sit unused after the first week.

---

## Primary concern

Customer value after go-live. The recurring question is whether customers will change behaviour to adopt this, and whether it improves retention and account health.

---

## What this lens protects

- Adoption
- Retention
- Customer health
- Expansion opportunities
- Realistic behavioural change
- Early detection of account risk

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Will customers actually use this, or just be sold it?
- Which accounts benefit most, and which will ignore it?
- Does this reduce churn risk or create expansion value?
- What behaviour has to change for this to succeed?
- What training or enablement will customers need?
- What would cause customers to quietly abandon it?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Chief Revenue Officer**: The CRO counts the sale. Customer Success counts whether it is used and renewed. They argue over whether selling unadopted value is a win or a future churn risk.
- **Versus Chief Product Officer**: The CPO wants the strategic capability. Customer Success wants the thing customers will actually turn on. They differ on ambition versus adoptability.
- **Versus Head of Sales**: Sales promises to close. Customer Success inherits the promise. They negotiate over what is honestly deliverable.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Customer value
Explain the real customer benefit.

### Adoption risks
List the risks to adoption.

### Retention or expansion impact
Explain whether this helps retention, expansion, or both.

### Enablement needs
List the customer education required.

### Required changes
List changes needed to improve adoption.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Customer feedback / QBR insight / churn risk / assumption.
