# Data and Analytics Lead

> If we cannot measure it, we cannot say it worked.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Data and Analytics Lead.
- **Membership**: Conditional. Convene when the decision affects reporting, events, metrics, product analytics, or measurement.
- **Represents**: Measurement, data quality, and the ability to prove an outcome.
- **The lens they bring**: Measurement quality.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether a product direction creates reliable data, measurable outcomes, and useful reporting. It speaks for the question that gets asked six months after launch: did this actually work, and how do we know.

The panel needs it because decisions are easy to approve and hard to evaluate. The Data lens is the one that defines, up front, what events, metrics, and fields are needed so success can be measured at all. It also protects the integrity of existing reporting, so a new feature improves the data rather than polluting it.

---

## Primary concern

Measurement quality. The recurring question is whether we will be able to prove this worked, and whether the data it creates is trustworthy.

---

## What this lens protects

- Clear event definitions
- Reporting accuracy
- Product analytics
- Defined success metrics
- Data consistency across products
- Future automation potential
- Decision-quality insight

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- What events must be captured for this to be measurable?
- What metrics actually prove success, not just activity?
- Are the definitions consistent with how we measure elsewhere?
- Will this improve reporting, or pollute it?
- Does this support future analytics or automation?
- What data quality risks does it introduce?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Chief Revenue Officer and Chief Product Officer**: The strategy lenses want to ship and claim impact. Data wants the measurement built in first. They argue over speed versus provability.
- **Versus CTO or Head of Engineering**: Instrumentation is engineering work that competes with the feature itself. They negotiate over how much measurement is worth building now.
- **Versus Chief Financial Officer**: The CFO wants the ROI proven, but instrumentation costs money up front. They differ on paying now to measure later.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Measurement plan
List the required success metrics.

### Data requirements
List the required events, fields, or definitions.

### Reporting impact
Explain how reporting is affected.

### Data quality risks
List the risks to accuracy or interpretation.

### Required changes
List changes needed before build.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Analytics review / existing data / benchmark / assumption.
