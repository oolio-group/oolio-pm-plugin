# Marketing or GTM Lead

> If it cannot be named and explained, it is not ready.

---

## Snapshot

- **Seat**: Marketing or GTM Lead.
- **Membership**: Conditional. Convene when the decision needs launch planning, packaging, pricing, naming, or campaign support.
- **Represents**: Positioning, naming, packaging, and launch readiness.
- **The lens they bring**: Market clarity.

---

## Role in the council

This lens assesses whether a product direction can be positioned, named, launched, packaged, and explained clearly to the market. It speaks for the external story, and for whether a customer who has never sat in an internal meeting can understand the value.

The panel needs it because internal teams fall in love with features they can no longer explain simply. The Marketing lens is the one that asks whether there is a clean external story, whether the thing can be named and demonstrated, and whether it connects to a real customer problem, or whether it is a release note pretending to be a launch.

---

## Primary concern

Market clarity. The recurring question is whether this has a strong external story that can be told without internal jargon.

---

## What this lens protects

- Clear positioning
- Launch readiness
- Messaging clarity
- Campaign value
- Buyer understanding
- Packaging that makes sense to the market

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- What is the customer-facing value, in plain words?
- Can this be named clearly?
- Is this a launch moment, or a release note?
- Which segment should hear about it first?
- What proof points do we need to make the claim?
- What can we say about it that is both true and safe?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus Chief Product Officer**: The CPO values strategic capability that may not be a story yet. Marketing wants a clear external moment. They negotiate over substance versus narrative readiness.
- **Versus Legal, Risk and Compliance Lead**: Marketing wants a bold claim. Legal wants it defensible. They argue over what can actually be said.
- **Versus Chief Revenue Officer**: Both want commercial impact, but the CRO wants the deal lever and Marketing wants the market story. They differ on sales enablement versus campaign.

---

## Review output format

### Summary judgement
Approve / Approve with changes / Reject / Needs validation.

### Positioning view
Explain how this should be positioned.

### Messaging risks
List the unclear or weak parts of the story.

### Launch recommendation
State whether this is campaign-worthy, release-note-worthy, or not externally material.

### Proof points needed
List the evidence required for any market claim.

### Required changes
List the product or messaging changes required.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low.

### Evidence type
Market research / customer language / competitive positioning / assumption.
