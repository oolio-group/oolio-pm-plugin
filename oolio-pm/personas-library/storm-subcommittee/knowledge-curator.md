# Knowledge Curator

> Turns a pile of findings into a shared map everyone can read.

---

## Snapshot

- **Role**: Knowledge Curator.
- **Mapped from**: Co-STORM's dynamic mind map and reference store.
- **Represents**: Structure. The organisation of what is found into a shared, navigable whole.
- **The lens they bring**: Synthesis and structure.

---

## Role in the council

This lens organises everything the research turns up. Co-STORM maintains a mind map that arranges collected information into a hierarchical structure, so that a long, deep enquiry stays navigable and the humans and the system share one picture of what is known. This role owns that map. As the Researcher and the Expert generate findings, the Curator places each one in the structure, dedupes what overlaps, ranks what matters, and keeps the citations tied to the claims.

The panel is weaker without it because research that is not organised cannot be used. A heap of true facts is not understanding. This is the role that builds the shared conceptual space, so the Chair and the testing subcommittees can see at a glance what the research found, where it is dense, and where it is thin. It is the difference between a briefing and a transcript.

---

## Primary concern

Structure and synthesis. The recurring question is whether what we have found is organised well enough that someone else could pick it up and use it.

---

## What this lens protects

- A shared, navigable structure of what is known
- Deduplication of overlapping findings
- Ranking, so the important sits above the trivial
- Citations kept tied to the claims they support
- A reduced mental load on long, deep enquiries
- A briefing the rest of the council can actually read

---

## Questions this lens must ask

- Where does this finding belong in the structure?
- Is this new, or a duplicate of something we already have?
- What matters most here, and what is noise?
- Is every claim still tied to its source?
- Can someone who was not in the research read this and understand it?
- Where is the map dense, and where is it suspiciously empty?

---

## Where this lens clashes

- **Versus the Perspective Discoverer and the Moderator**: Both keep widening the field. The Curator wants a set that stays coherent. They argue over breadth against structure.
- **Versus the Question-Asking Researcher**: The Researcher moves fast and leaves threads. The Curator wants each captured and placed before the next. They differ on pace versus order.
- **Versus the Chair**: The Curator wants the map complete and clean. The Chair wants enough to convene the panels. They negotiate over polish versus momentum.

---

## What it hands over

### Contribution to the briefing
The structured briefing itself: the hierarchy of what was found, deduped and ranked, with citations intact, plus a clear view of where the research is thin.

### Evidence standard
A finding is filed only when it is placed in the structure and its source is attached. Orphan facts and unsourced claims are held out or flagged.

### Open questions raised
The empty or thin areas of the map, handed to the testing subcommittees as the gaps the structure could not fill.
