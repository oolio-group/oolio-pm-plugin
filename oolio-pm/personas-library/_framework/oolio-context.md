# Oolio context

Why this file exists: every persona must be grounded in what Oolio actually is and where Oolio is going. This is the working brief.

If something in this file changes (a new brand joins the group, a new geography, a new line of business), update this file first and then sweep the persona library.

---

## What Oolio is, today

Oolio is a hospitality technology group. Headquartered in Australia, with operations in Australia, New Zealand, the United Kingdom, the United States, and Denmark. Backed by Pemba Capital Partners. Roughly 22,000 venues on the platform across the group.

Formed in 2022 when five established Australian hospitality technology companies came together. The brand portfolio inside Oolio is the most important part of understanding the business.

### The brand portfolio

| Brand | Where it lives | Typical venue |
|---|---|---|
| **bepoz** | Pubs, hotels, clubs, restaurants | Multi-revenue-centre venues, gaming, accommodation |
| **deliverit** | Takeaway, pizza | Single and multi-site pizza and takeaway operators |
| **idealpos** | Restaurants, cafes, tourism parks | Broad mid-market hospitality |
| **ordermate** | Fine dining, cafes, bars | Table management, at-table ordering, service-led venues |
| **OOLIO platform** | Casual dining, takeaway | All-in-one POS and payments, intuitive front and back of house |
| **swiftpos** | Gaming venues, clubs, stadia, convention centres | Complex multi-outlet, high-throughput |
| **Oolio Pay** | All of the above | Integrated payments |

Each brand carries decades of vertical expertise. The Oolio group does not yet present as a single product. Personas should reflect the brand a customer is actually on, because that is the system they are touching every shift.

## What Oolio wants to be, tomorrow

This is the strategic direction the persona library has to serve, not the public website language.

Oolio's stated direction is to move from "a point of sale company" to a **hospitality technology partner and subject matter expert**. The shift has three parts.

1. **From product to platform.** A single Oolio platform across the brand portfolio, not five separate vendors under one logo.
2. **From technology to expertise.** Oolio's people have run venues. That expertise becomes a productised consultancy offer that helps clients grow, not just operate.
3. **From vendor to growth partner.** Customers do not just buy a till and payments. They buy advice, benchmarking, operational guidance, and a roadmap.

This matters for personas because:

- Decision-makers (owners, COOs, GMs) become buyers of advice and outcomes, not just kit. The persona files should reflect what they want to be told, not just what they want to buy.
- Operators (FOH managers, head chefs, bar managers) are the people whose lives the consultancy improves. Their pain points are the evidence the consultancy works.
- Front-line users (bartenders, line cooks, servers) are the experience the customer feels. If the platform fails for them, the consultancy and the brand fail.

---

## What the persona library has to do for this shift

Three jobs.

1. **Anchor product decisions.** Every roadmap and PRD must explicitly name the personas it serves and the personas it costs.
2. **Anchor GTM and exec narrative.** Every one-pager, deck, board update, and customer story refers to named personas, not "the operator".
3. **Anchor the consultancy offer.** Each consultancy engagement should be diagnosable persona by persona. The library is the diagnostic frame.
