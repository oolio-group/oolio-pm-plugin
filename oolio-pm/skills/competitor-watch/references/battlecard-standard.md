# competitor-watch — battlecard standard (reference)

The sales-facing output. One page per competitor, built from the dossier and win-loss patterns, published to Confluence on approval. The design constraints, in order: a rep must find the answer in ten seconds; every claim must be current-dated or the whole card loses trust; honesty about their strengths or the first lost deal discredits it.

## The Fact-Impact-Act rule

Every entry carries three parts: the competitive **fact** (specific, dated, sourced), why it matters to the **buyer** (impact), and exactly what the rep should say or ask next (**act**). "They're expensive" fails; "Lightspeed moved AU merchants to forced in-house payments in 2025, tripling fees for holdouts (verified Jul 2026) — ask: 'what did your last processing statement look like, and what happens to your rate at renewal?'" works.

## Card skeleton

```
# Battlecard — <Competitor>          Verified current as of <date>

## In ten seconds
Two sentences: who they are, the one thing to know this quarter.

## Where they win (honest)
3-4 bullets. What they genuinely do better, dated. Reps lose credibility
(and deals) discovering these live.

## Where they're weak
3-4 bullets, Fact-Impact-Act framed, from dossier weakness themes and
win-loss patterns. Structural weaknesses (contract terms, business model)
over cosmetic ones.

## How we win
3-4 bullets. Every Oolio capability claim verified against oolio-context.md,
products/, or a connected source. Unverified counter = flagged gap, not a bullet.

## Landmines
Questions to plant that expose their gaps ("ask them what the exit fee is
at month 18"). Their traps to expect, and the rebuttal.

## Objections and rebuttals
The 3-5 objections reps actually hear (from win-loss), each with a
one-line rebuttal and a proof point.

## Proof points
Named, verifiable: reference customers, published numbers, dated events.
Nothing invented, nothing undated.

---
Stale or wrong? Comment on this page — cards update on detected change,
not on a calendar. Source dossier: <Brain link>.
```

## Rules

- **Derived, not researched.** The card is a view over the dossier and win-loss patterns. Thin dossier → run dossier mode first; never research directly into a card.
- **Dated throughout.** The card header carries "Verified current as of"; any individually older claim carries its own date. One stale fact kills the card's trust.
- **Oolio claims verified or flagged.** No fabricated counter-capabilities, ever (house rule: no fabricated Oolio facts). A weakness with no verified counter still goes on the card; the counter is marked as a gap for the human to fill.
- **Update on trigger, not calendar.** A high-significance sweep change touching a card flags it for update the same week. The quarterly freshness audit re-dates the rest.
- **Distribution beats existence.** On publish, remind the owner: the card lives where reps work (linked from the CRM/deal workflow), and the feedback loop is the page comments. A card nobody sees in-deal is shelf-ware.
- **Internal only.** Cards contain competitor-critical framing; Confluence internal spaces only, never a public or customer-visible surface.
